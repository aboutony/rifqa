# RIFQA / رفقة Privacy Policy Draft

Status: Draft for legal review  
Jurisdiction target: Kingdom of Saudi Arabia  
Primary compliance reference: Saudi Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL), its Implementing Regulations, and SDAIA guidance.

## 1. Who We Are

RIFQA / رفقة is a maternal wellness companion designed to support pregnancy, postpartum, and early motherhood. The app provides tracking, reminders, educational support, AI-assisted guidance, and wellness recommendations.

RIFQA is not an emergency service, hospital, clinic, insurer, or replacement for licensed medical care.

## 2. Data We Collect

We collect only what is needed to provide the service:

- Account and authentication data, such as anonymous account ID, email, or phone number if the user chooses to add them.
- Pregnancy and motherhood profile data, such as pregnancy week, due date, postpartum stage, child age, and preferences.
- Health and wellness logs, such as mood, sleep, symptoms, weight, kick counts, contraction sessions, exercise plans, relaxation preferences, and notes.
- AI interaction data needed to generate support responses, recommendations, and visit summaries.
- Consent and privacy request records.
- Technical data, such as device/app events, crash logs, and security events.

We should avoid collecting national ID, employer details, precise location, payment data, or identifiable family-member data unless a future feature strictly requires it and separate consent is obtained.

## 3. Sensitive Personal Data

Pregnancy, health, mental wellness, symptoms, and related records may be sensitive personal data. RIFQA must treat this data with stricter safeguards, limited access, clear consent, and minimized processing.

## 4. Why We Process Data

We process data to:

- Provide pregnancy, postpartum, and child-development tracking.
- Generate wellness recommendations, such as relaxation audio, prayer/recitation, breathing, and safe movement prompts.
- Store doctor-provided exercise instructions when the user enters them.
- Help prepare doctor visit summaries.
- Improve safety flows and identify urgent guidance needs.
- Provide privacy controls, export, and deletion handling.
- Maintain security, reliability, and abuse prevention.

## 5. AI Processing

RIFQA may use AI to generate supportive responses and recommendations. AI output is informational and supportive only. It must not diagnose, prescribe medication, replace a clinician, or override doctor instructions.

For exercise, doctor instructions always override AI recommendations. If no doctor plan is stored, AI may suggest conservative wellness actions only when no concerning symptoms are detected.

For urgent signs such as bleeding, severe pain, clearly reduced fetal movement, or unsafe/self-harm feelings, deterministic safety rules should direct the user to emergency or clinician support instead of relying on AI.

## 6. Legal Basis and Consent

RIFQA should obtain clear consent before processing sensitive health and wellness data. The user must be able to withdraw optional consents where applicable.

The app should show consent and medical disclaimer notices during onboarding, in settings, in AI chat, and in emergency/safety flows.

## 7. Data Minimization

RIFQA should collect the minimum data necessary for each feature. Anonymous or low-PII use should be available where practical.

## 8. Data Sharing

We do not sell personal data.

We may share data only with:

- Service providers needed to operate the app, such as hosting, database, authentication, notifications, crash reporting, and AI processing providers.
- Healthcare providers or trusted contacts only when the user chooses to share a summary or record.
- Authorities if legally required.

Employers, sponsors, or B2B partners must never receive individual-level health, mood, AI-chat, pregnancy, or behavior data.

## 9. International Transfers

If any data is processed or stored outside Saudi Arabia, RIFQA must assess and document the transfer under PDPL and applicable SDAIA rules. Sensitive data transfers require special care and legal review.

## 10. Data Retention

RIFQA should retain personal data only as long as needed for the service, legal obligations, security, or user-requested archive features. Users should be able to request deletion.

## 11. User Rights

Subject to applicable law, users should be able to:

- Access their data.
- Correct inaccurate data.
- Request export.
- Request deletion.
- Withdraw optional consent.
- Understand why data is collected and how it is used.

## 12. Security

RIFQA should use safeguards such as:

- Row-level security for user-owned records.
- Server-side API access for privileged operations.
- No service-role keys in client code.
- Secret management through environment variables.
- Audit records for consent and safety events.
- Restricted access to production data.

## 13. Children

RIFQA is intended for mothers and adult caregivers. Features involving child records should be controlled by the parent/caregiver and should minimize child-identifying data.
