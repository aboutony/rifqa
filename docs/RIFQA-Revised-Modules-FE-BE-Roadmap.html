1. Blunt Strategic Verdict

RIFQA has a strong strategic spine: Arabic-first, Saudi-first, privacy-first, emotionally intelligent maternal support. But the product cannot become number one while missing basic pregnancy utilities. Contraction counter, kick counter, pregnancy weight tracking, baby size comparisons, visuals, symptom logs, milestones, and memory capture are the entry ticket.

The winning product is not tracker vs companion. It must be both: the best tracker, the safest companion, and the most culturally fluent maternal platform in the region.

5. Recommended MVP Cut

MVP must include the table-stakes toolkit before public launch.

  1. Onboarding, privacy promise, anonymous or low-PII start.
  2. Journey profile, pregnancy week/due date, unknown-week flow.
  3. Home dashboard, daily emotional check-in, PPD/anxiety screening, crisis-safe mode.
  4. AI companion, what-to-do-now cards, Saudi care pathway.
  5. Contraction counter, kick counter, weight tracker, symptoms, medications.
  6. Baby size comparisons, weekly visuals, feeding/sleep/diaper logs.
  7. Privacy center, notifications, subscriptions with crisis protections, CMS, admin console.

Do not include open community, full marketplace, insurer integrations, complex gamification, or unsupported medical claims in MVP.

10. Non-Negotiable Product Rules

  1. Do not launch publicly without contraction counter, kick counter, and weight tracking.
  2. Do not paywall crisis, distress, or urgent guidance.
  3. Do not show diagnostic labels to users.
  4. Do not store raw chat content for analytics.
  5. Do not give employers individual-level visibility.
  6. Do not treat Arabic as translation. Arabic is the default product.
  7. Do not create an open community without moderation infrastructure.
  8. Do not make the UI feel like a hospital form.
  9. Do not let AI make safety-critical decisions alone.
  10. Do not overbuild marketplace/community before the core companion works.

Number-one app bet: RIFQA must become the first app that understands the Saudi mother as a whole person: body, baby, emotions, faith, family, healthcare system, and privacy.
